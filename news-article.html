Achieving a Better Night’s Sleep

By: John Kelly, Marketing Consultant

April 29, 2016  |  St. Vincent Carmel Hospital

Share this article

Inspire Therapy offers an alternative to CPAP for obstructive sleep apnea

Are you getting enough sleep? If not, do you know why? Sleep is critically important for good health, including cardiovascular health, yet a National Sleep Foundation survey indicates that 60 percent of Americans have sleep problems. Of those, many suffer from sleep disorders, such as sleep apnea.

Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA) is the most common form of apnea and occurs when the tongue and other soft tissues relax during sleep and obstruct a person’s airway. If you have OSA, your brain senses a problem and arouses you from sleep long enough to clear the airway, but only for a few breaths, and then the cycle continues. OSA can lead to high blood pressure, heart disease and stroke, among other health risks.

St.Vincent is excited to offer an FDA-approved surgical option for OSA: Inspire therapy hypoglossal nerve stimulator implantation. Inspire therapy is an option for patients with moderate to severe OSA who cannot use or experience discomfort using a continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP) machine. Inspire therapy is a fully implanted system that delivers mild stimulation to keep a patient’s airway open during sleep and is controlled with a small handheld sleep remote.

St.Vincent is the only hospital in Indiana trained in the implantation of this hypoglossal nerve stimulator implant.

About St. Vincent Indianapolis: Driven by the faith of four Daughters of Charity who arrived in Indianapolis in 1881 with $34.77 in their pockets, the St. Vincent Indianapolis mission is to provide the highest level of care possible to those who are struggling by following our Core Values of Reverence, Integrity, Wisdom, Creativity, Service of the Poor and Dedication. Our healthcare ministry has grown to include: Women's Services, Pediatrics through Peyton Manning Children’s Hospital at St. Vincent, Orthopedics, Cardiovascular, Neuroscience, Cancer Care and Trauma. The ageless mission of St. Vincent remains unchanged: to minister to the minds, bodies and spirits of those in our care.

About Ascension: Ascension is a Catholic healthcare organization dedicated to the transformation of healthcare through excellent clinical care throughout the continuum of care and through innovation. Ascension is the nation’s largest Catholic and non-profit health system, providing the highest quality care to all with special attention to those who are poor and vulnerable. Last year, Ascension provided $1.8 billion in care for persons living in poverty in addition to community benefit programs. Its Mission-focused Health Ministries employ more than 153,000 associates serving in more than 1,900 sites of care in 23 states and the District of Columbia. Ascension’s direct subsidiaries provide services that include healthcare delivery, medical equipment management, treasury management, resource and supply management, venture capital investing, physician practice management, and risk management.
